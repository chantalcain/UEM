A great crew of guys for the job!

The UEM management team consists of over 100 years of collective experience in new work, maintenance and environmental dredging projects covering the majority of the world. Listed below are experience summaries of the principal management team:

Brian Lofgren, President of UEM

Mr. Lofgren started in the dredging and construction business in 1982 working as an equipment operator, boatman, surveyor, fabricator and dredger man. Mr. Lofgren progressively was promoted to Leverman, Foreman and Superintendent. The companies Mr. Lofgren worked for over the years were: Western Pacific Dredging, Great Lakes Dredge and Dock, Ross Island Sand and Gravel, John L. Jersey and Son, Manson Construction and Wilder Construction Company (now Granite Construction Company). Mr. Lofgren experienced projects from the Panama Canal to Prudhoe Bay, Alaska. Collectively, Mr. Lofgren has participated on over 200 million dollars of projects.

Robert L. Lofgren, Engineering and Technical Support

Mr. R. L. Lofgren has over 50 years of experience in the dredging business throughout the world. Mr. Lofgren has a BS in Civil Engineering, Oregon State University and MS Civil Engineering University of California Berkeley. Robert (Bob) is a world renowned expert in dredging, applying ingenuity to all types of dredging projects. Bob's early years experienced working as a boatman, leverman, surveyor mate on the steam powered units dredging the lower Columbia River. Following achievement of his formal education, Bob was the Senior Vice President of Western Pacific Dredging with projects from a Dam in Brazil, Tigris Euphrates River in Iraq to the Mt. St. Helens Restoration Projects on the Cowlitz and Columbia Rivers.

Mark T. Riem, Operations and Business Management

Mr. Riem's career initiated in the heavy civil/structural/mechanical type projects to heavy marine/dredging projects and onto environmental construction/remediation and environmental dredging/capping projects. Started the career as a metal fabricator, demolition-railway laborer/welder, and heavy equipment operator; earned college degree, office engineer at initial full time engagement in the industry then progressively promoted to Construction/Branch Manager.
In addition to project operation oversight of the branch/area(s), actively engaged in contract negotiations, business planning/budgeting/work prospectus, construction/dredging feasibility studies, business development, Design/Build project proposals, estimating and bidding processes. Trained and developed many entry level personnel in the basics of the contracting business.

Over the 30+ year work experience period, projects bid, supervised and constructed covered a diverse range of construction types over a broad geographic area with an emphasis on heavy earthwork, utilities and marine related disciplines. The volume of project experience is approximately 320+ Million dollars in revenue over 100+ contracts. Project sizes ranged from $50,000.00 to $146,000,000.00. Company employers were: The Kiewit Companies, Riedel Companies, Wilder Construction (now Granite) and Parsons.
